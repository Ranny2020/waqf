The Beginning of Tikau Zakat and Waqf Foundation (2002)

Tikau Zakat and Waqf Foundation came into being in 2002; as a result of an executive directives by the first civilian Governor of Yobe state Alhaji Bukar Abba Ibrahim, FNIQS, who directed all the fourteen Emirate councils in the state to establish a committee of Zakat and Waqf in their various domains; in an effort by Yobe State Government to establish Shari'ah Law in the state.
In compliance with the said executive directives, His Royal Highness the Mai of Tikau, Alhaji Muhammadu Abubakar Ibn Grema Constituted and inaugurated a seven man high powered committee with him as the grand patron and Galadima of Tikau Alhaji Sambo Sarki as chairman together with six other prominent members to implement the collection and distribution of Zakat; and also to Lanuch an annual Waqf collection

The History and Achievement of Tikau Zakat and Waqf Foundation

Tikau Zakat and Waqf Foundation was one of the first set of Emirate to have lanuched its Zakat and Waqf after the lanuching of the state's chapter. The lanuching was organized by a very powerful committee under the leadership of Late Emir, that set our modalities in which the chapter was lanuched. All the Tikau(emirates) prominent sons and daughter within and outside the state were called upon to give out their zakat and waqf, many answered the called. The lanuching was held at both the emirate and districts level where all four districts had their lanuching as follows:

  1. DAWASA DISTRICT:This district lanuched its own Zakat and Waqf on the 10th Feb, 2002. At Lanuching, the sum of 111,000.00 Naira was realized with 22bags of grain, a bag of beans and a cow was collected
  2. LANGAWA DISTRICT: Langawa district chapter lanuched at degubi. The district's headquarters on 17th Feb, 2002. The sum of 98,000.00 Naira and sixteen (16) bags of grain was realized from the district
  3. DAZIGAU DISTRICT: Theirs was lanuched on the 21st Feb, 2002 in which the total sum of 554,000.00 was realized together with 20 bags of grain.
  4. NANGERE DISTRICT:On 28th Feb, 2002. Nangere district chapter was held at Sabon Garin and the sum of 486,740.00 was realized with 18 bags of grain and a cow.

Our Principles

Stronger, United Ummah

Transparency

Tikau Waqf Foundation aims to be transparent as it is essential for public organizations to build trust, demonstrate accountability, and provide a clear explanantion of How funds are used to achieve the organization's charitable mission.

Professionalism

Tikau Waqf Foundation maintains professionalism as it is vital for establishing credibility, ensuring efficient operations, and fostering public trust, which are essential elements for sustained impact and support.

compliance

compliance serves as a cornerstone of effective charity management; as it upjolds legal standards, fosters public trust, and ensures continued support, thereby protecting the organization's reputation and ability to deliver its charitable commitments.

Independence

Preserving independence is essential for charities to maintain autonomous decision-making, avoid conflicts of interest, and uphold their commitment to unbiased, mission-driven initiatives thereby strengthening credibility and public trust.
