H.R.H Alhaji Abubakar Muhammadu Talba Ibn Grema (The Grand Patron)

The Mai of Tikau, who has a bachelor degree in Agric Economics and Extension, from Bayero University Kano, and Msc in Agric Economics from University of Readings, United Kingdom (Applied Econometrics, Development and Resource Economics) and a PhD from Yobe State University (inview) in Rural Development. He was the Deputy Director Agric Services, at Yobe State University and Also the District Head of Sabon Gari and the Yarima of Tikau. An educated scholar is deeply committed to knowledge and critical thinking.

Alhaji Muhammadu Sambo Sarki (The Chairman of the Committe)

Alhaji Muhammadu Sambo Sarki, born in Degubi Village, Yobe State, began his education at Nangere Primary School and later attended Borno Teachers College, earning his Grade 11 certificate. He served as a teacher and principal in various schools, culminating in his role as Principal of Fika Government School, from which he retired in 2010. Following his father's death, he became the village head of Degubi in 1998 and later the district head, while also serving as the Chairman of the Zakat and Waqf committee established by the late Mai of Tikau.

Alhaji Suleiman A. Isa (The Secretary of the Committee)

Alhaji Suleiman A. Isa was born in Dawasa, Yobe State, and began his education at Chukuriwa Primary School before attending Teachers College Gashua and Potiskum, where he specialized in Arabic and Islamic Education. He has also studied at the Sheik (Dr) Ibrahim El-Tayeb Institute and the University of Maiduguri, and he completed a Cadet Inspectorate course focusing on citizenship and leadership. After a career as a teacher and various roles in the police force, he became a village head and held leadership positions such as Chairman of the Dawasa Sharia Implementation Committee and the Board of Trustees for SHIS Potiskum; he currently serves as the Walin and Kaigama of Tikau Emirate.

Muhammad Audu Kukuri (Chairman of Mallam's)

Mohammad Audu Kukuri was born in Kukuri Village, Nangere, and began his education at Kukuri Primary School before attending Arabic Teachers College Gombe from 1969 to 1973, where he became a Grade II teacher. He was employed at Kukuri Primary School on August 1, 1973, and served in various roles, including headmaster and assistant headmaster, until his retirement on August 1, 2002. In 2013, he was appointed to the Tikau Zakat and Waqf Foundation committee for his extensive knowledge of Islam and currently serves as the secretary of the inheritance distribution committee in Tikau, promoting Islamic values and community betterment.

Alaramma Malam Uwaisu (Member of the Committee)

Alaramma Malam Uwaizu Idriss was born in Nangere. After his father's passing, he returned home to continue his studies and took over the teaching of the Quran and Islamic texts. He gained a large following of students at his school, becoming renowned for his extensive knowledge.
Following the death of Alhaji Malam Yahaya, he was appointed as the Imam of Jumaa (Friday prayers) in Nangere town by the Tikau Emirate Council, alongside other esteemed individuals. His knowledge and piety led the late esteemed scholar, Alhaji Muhammad Abubakar Ibn Grema, to verify his qualifications and appoint him as a member of the important Tikau Zakat and Waqf committee.

Alkali Muhammad Ali Sabo (Member of the Committee)

Alkali Muhammad Ali Sabo was born in Dorowa Dodi, Nangere, and attended Kukorgadu Primary School before studying at Bama Teachers' College and Arabic Teachers' College. He furthered his education with a Diploma in Sharia and Civil Law from Borno College of Legal and Islamic Studies and earned a Bachelor of Arts in Arabic and Islamic Studies from the University of Maiduguri. Sabo began his career as a class teacher in 1979, later serving in various judicial roles, including Sharia Court Judge and Chief Inspector of Sharia Court Judges, until his retirement in 2013; he was appointed to the Tikau Zakat and Waqf Foundation in 2014 for his extensive legal and Arabic knowledge.

AbdulKarim Inuwa (The Zakat Collector)

AbdulKarim Inuwa was born in Tikau town, Nangere Local Government Area. He began his education at Nangere Primary School from 1973 to 1979 and furthered his studies at Potiskum Teachers College to enhance his teaching skills. After completing his education, he secured a government position as a secretary in the Tikau district office on May 1, 1988, later becoming the secretary for the Nangere Local Government Chairman.
His dedication caught the attention of the Emir of Tikau, who appointed him as the Zakat collection officer for the Tikau Zakat and Waqf Foundation. In this role, he was responsible for collecting Zakat from all districts within the Tikau Emirate and received high commendations for his efforts. AbdulKarim retired from government service on May 1, 2023, but continues to serve as the chief officer for Zakat collection in the committee, reflecting his commitment and hard work.

Malam Muhammadu Kabiru Ali (Member of the Committee)

Malam Muhammadu Kabiru Ali was born in the ancient town of Nangere, Yobe State. He began his education at a traditional Islamic school (Makarantar Allo) under his father's guidance and later attended Darus Salafiyya Primary School in Potiskum, completing his studies between 2001 and 2003. He continued his Islamic education at Annur Institute for Islamic Education in Kano (Potiskum branch) from 2003 to 2005, and furthered his studies at Attatur-Rasheed Institute for Islamic Education, affiliated with Ahmadu Bello University Zaria, from 2014 to 2016.
After formal education, Malam Muhammadu Kabiru Ali sought knowledge from renowned scholars and extensively studied jurisprudence (fiqh) and hadith. His expertise in Quranic recitation and teaching earned him recognition, leading the late Emir of Tikau to include him in the program for delivering sermons at the Friday Mosque. He was appointed to the Sharia Committee and the Conciliation Committee to help resolve community disputes, and he is also a member of the Emirate's Inheritance Distribution Committee. In 2024, he was appointed to the Tikau Zakat and Waqf Foundation committee, further solidifying his role in community service and Islamic education.
