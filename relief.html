Economic Relief & Empowerment

Addressing Muslim economic needs: immediate assistance and sustainable growth

Why is it important?

Building bridges of hope, one Waqf at a time.

Supporting economic relief and empowerment is crucial for establishing a sustainable path to financial independence. By funding organiztions focused on economic development, we empower individuals to create opportunities, fostering self-reliance, resilience, and a lasting escape from poverty.

Economic relief organizations can drive swift change with financial support. this enables prompt response of crises, delivering vital aid, training, and resources to those in need, making a tangible difference.

Economic empowerment programs with educational components enhance financial literacy, vocational skills, and entrepreneurial abilities. As individuals gain economic independence, they contribute more meaningfully to society, foestering a positive feedback loop of economic growth, social engagement, and community uplift.

Utimately, economic relief and empowerment initiatives empower the Muslim community to thrive, breaking cycles of poverty and dependency. By promoting financial independence, literacy, and civic engagement, we foster a stronger, more resilient community.

How Waqf are being used.

Tikau Humanitarian Projects

The Tikau Foundation engage in some humanitarian projects that enhance and promote both the social and religious life of the communities such as the repairs of well (on the request of the community for assistance) at Tikau village after it was damaged by rainstorm.
The community of Tashan Dorawa built a Mosque for daily prayers through direct labour with the financial assistance & supervision of Zakat and Waqf Committee.

Other Donations were:

  1. Kukuri Islamiya school project
  2. Government Secondary school, Nangere
  3. Mosque project at Garin Idi Barde (in Fune L.G.A)
