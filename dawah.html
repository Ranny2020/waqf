Da'wah Religious Awareness and Outreach

Fostering a nuanced understanding of Islamic teachings and practices

Why is it important?

Nurturing a generation of change-makers

Islam is a stunning faith, rooted in the profound declaration that 'there is no dignity worthy of veneration and worship except Allah.' This powerful affirmation is the cornerstone of islamic belief, emphasizing the oneness of God and guiding principles of compassion, justice, and peace.

At Tikau Zakat and Waqf Foundation, our mission is to share the timeless wisdom of Islam with the world, fostering greater understanding and respect among people of diverse faiths. By bridging gaps and dispelling misconceptions, we aim to reduce conflicts rooted in religious misunderstandings. Through our efforts, we strive to cultivate a sense of community, shared values, and social cohesion, ultimately strengthening bonds and promoting mutual support among individuals from all walks of life.

Tikau Enlightment Programme

The Committee board decided to call people learned in islamic knowledge from different part of Tikau and outside, an interview was conducted for over 40 islamic preacher where 24 was selected as qualified preachers and posted to 24 juma'at mosques for 2 weeks preaching in every month based on guided areas in other to facilitate basic islamic knowledge. The areas include those on purification, ritual bathing, knowing how to perform ablution, prayers and to understand more about kabli and ba'adi e.t.c
Also under this programme, a preacher is posted to a particular mosque for a period of three months after which he will be transferred to another mosque for the same period. At the end of every three month, a meeting is held with the imams of the juma'at mosques, together with preachers as well as the 13 village heads to discuss the progress and hitches experienced under the supervision of the committee board.

Tikau Oral Teaching

The Tikau Zakat and Waqf Committee has introduced Oral teaching in hundred (100) regukar Mosques out of the three hundred and ninety three (393) registered one in the emirate. The objective is to educate the followers of the imams on some basic Islamic Knowledge concerning their daily worship.
More than two hundred (200) copies of Ahdhari and Ashmawi were brought and distributed to the imams of the selected Mosques.
